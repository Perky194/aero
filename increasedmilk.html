Why Milk Quality is Necessary

Maintaining high milk quality is essential to safeguard public health, enhance shelf life, meet standards, build trust, and unlock better market opportunities for farmers.

Importance of Milk Quality

Quality milk reduces spoilage and contamination risks, ensures nutritional value, earns premium prices, and strengthens the dairy industry's sustainability and reputation.

Steps to Maintain and Improve Milk Quality

1. Clean Milking Practices

  • Udder Preparation: Clean and dry udder using sanitizing wipes or clean warm water.
  • Milker Hygiene: Wash hands before and during milking.
  • Milking Equipment: Sterilize machines, buckets, strainers before use.
  • Immediate Cooling: Cool milk ASAP after milking to slow bacteria.

2. Proper Storage and Handling

  • Containers: Use clean, food-grade containers with lids.
  • Refrigeration: Store at ≤ 4 °C promptly.
  • Transport: Use insulated carriers to markets.
  • Reduce Holding Time: Minimize storage before processing.

3. Disease & Contamination Control

  • Mastitis Prevention: Regular monitoring and prompt treatment.
  • Parasite Control: Routine deworming and parasite management.
  • Water Quality: Provide clean drinking water.
  • Feed Safety: Avoid moldy or contaminated feeds.

4. Regular Testing & Monitoring

  • Milk Testing: Check for bacteria, somatic cells, adulterants.
  • Record Keeping: Track quality metrics and treatments.
  • Training: Teach best hygiene and handling practices.
