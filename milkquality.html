Why Increase Milk Production?

Boosting milk output strengthens farmer income, ensures food security, and enables investments in improved feed, breeding, and infrastructure.

Importance

  • Supports household nutrition and income.
  • Enhances ability to reinvest in farm productivity.
  • Supplies consistent volumes for processors, boosting market reputation.

Feeding Procedures

  • Balanced Diet: Provide good-quality Napier, Desmodium, plus concentrates for energy and protein.
  • Feeding Frequency: Offer three balanced feeds per day – before milking, midday, and after milking.
  • Seasonal Storage: Store silage or hay ahead of dry season for continuous quality feed.
  • Water & Minerals: Provide fresh water and mineral blocks (selenium, zinc, phosphorus) daily.

Health & Hygiene

  • Deworming & Tick Control: Deworm monthly and apply acaricides regularly.
  • Mastitis Prevention: Use teat dips after milking and check for udder health.
  • Clean Environment: Provide dry clean bedding and proper waste drainage.
  • Artificial Insemination: Train farmers to detect estrus and use proven genetics.

Monitoring & Adjustment

  • Milk Recording: Track daily milk volumes per cow.
  • Body Condition: Check and record condition monthly.
  • Feed Trials: Adjust rations if yields stall or drop.
  • Farmer Training: Hold monthly field sessions and peer reviews.
