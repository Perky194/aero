Nutrition & Supplements

Feeding for strength, growth, and production.

1. Early Calf Feeding

Nutrition starts within the first hour of life. Calves must receive **colostrum** to boost immunity. By the second week, introduce calf starters and clean water to support gut development.

2. Balanced Rationing

Dairy cows need the right balance of **carbohydrates, proteins, vitamins, and minerals**. A typical daily mix includes silage, hay, fresh fodder, grains, and mineral blocks. Feeding quantity depends on weight and production stage.

3. Seasonal Feeding Practices

During dry seasons, farmers must use preserved feeds like hay or maize silage. Supplement with molasses or urea-treated straw to maintain energy levels and avoid weight loss.

4. Mineral & Vitamin Supplements

Mineral licks, salt blocks, and injectable vitamins prevent fertility issues, milk fever, and growth disorders. Farmers are encouraged to track supplement intake especially during pregnancy and lactation periods.

5. Water Is a Nutrient

Clean, cool water must be available 24/7. A cow producing 20 liters of milk drinks about 80–120 liters daily. Dehydration reduces feed intake and milk production sharply.
