Many farmers in Nyandarua County face challenges accessing reliable and profitable dairy markets. Despite increased production, low milk prices and limited collection infrastructure reduce earnings. Without formal contracts or cooperative linkages, most farmers rely on middlemen offering exploitative prices.

  • Lack of cooling and storage centers causes spoilage during transport.
  • Absence of structured markets means dependence on brokers.
  • Poor road infrastructure limits market reach, especially during rainy seasons.
  • Limited knowledge of quality standards makes it hard to meet buyer requirements.

Farmers receive low prices and delayed payments, limiting their ability to invest in production. Spoiled milk results in direct losses. Without organized markets, farmers cannot plan, negotiate better terms, or build business resilience. On a broader scale, low milk volumes disrupt processor supply chains and discourage local dairy development.

AERO DAIRY has established milk aggregation points equipped with cooling tanks in key zones of Nyandarua. Farmers deliver milk collectively, preserving quality and reducing spoilage. We support bulk transport to major processors and negotiate competitive pricing through cooperatives.

Our digital marketing platform connects farmers to buyers directly, cutting off middlemen. We also provide training on milk quality standards, value addition (e.g., yogurt, ghee), and contract negotiation.

Through investment in road rehabilitation and partnerships with logistics providers, we facilitate all-season delivery to high-value markets.

These interventions have increased farmer incomes, strengthened trust in dairy markets, and improved Nyandarua's competitiveness in the national milk economy.
